How Credit Actually Works

If you thought your credit score was a secret society handshake you missed, welcome to the club. Here’s how the system really works, and why (spoiler) it’s not out to get you — but it might enjoy your misery.

The Score Haters

Your score is a mysterious number between 300 and 850 that somehow controls your financial life, your mortgage rates, and apparently how attractive you are to lenders. It’s made up of:

  • Payment History (35%) — Pay late once, and it’ll haunt you longer than that one ex.
  • Amounts Owed (30%) — Keep your card balances low or explain yourself to the robot overlords.
  • Length of Credit History (15%) — Older accounts get brownie points. Yes, more than your alma mater.
  • Credit Mix (10%) — Having a mortgage and a car loan makes you look responsible. Or in debt.
  • New Credit (10%) — Too many applications? Calm down, big spender. You’re desperate.

Sure, the formula is a mystery, but the math is simple: pay on time, don’t max out your cards, and keep those old accounts alive.
