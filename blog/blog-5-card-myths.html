5 Credit Card Myths That Need to Die (Right Now)

Welcome to the wonderful world of credit cards—the plastic rectangle people love to hate and hate to understand. Let's torch five old-school myths that keep people broke, stressed, or living in fear of their next billing cycle.

Myth #1: Carrying a Balance Boosts Your Score

Nope. Paying interest is not a prize. Your credit score loves it when you pay your bill in full, not when you gift the bank free money every month.

Myth #2: Closing Cards is Always Bad

Sure, closing an old account might ding your score a bit, but keeping fee-laden, unused cards “just for your score” makes no sense. If you’re done with a card (especially one with an annual fee), call it quits.

Myth #3: More Cards = More Debt

Having multiple cards just means more lines of credit—and a healthier score if you don’t spend like a caffeinated teenager. Self-control, people. It’s a vibe.

Myth #4: Rewards Cards Are a Trap

Only if you’re bad at math and discipline. Used right, rewards cards pay you for spending you’d do anyway. Used wrong, well… buy three years of ramen and call it a life lesson.

Myth #5: Applying for Cards Tanks Your Score Forever

A hard inquiry drops your score by a handful of points—and it bounces back. If you don’t apply for five cards a week, you’ll survive.

The Real Truth

Credit cards aren’t evil. They’re tools, and sometimes really profitable ones. Unpack the myths, pay attention, and you might actually come out ahead.

*No myths were harmed in the writing of this blog. Turns out, common sense works if you try it occasionally.
