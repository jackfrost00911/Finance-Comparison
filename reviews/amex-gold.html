American Express Gold Review 2025: The Card That Turns Your Food Addiction Into Rewards

Last updated: October 2025 | 9 min read

4.7/5

OUR RATING

The American Express Gold Card is Amex's love letter to people who spend ridiculous amounts of money on food. If you're the type who orders DoorDash three times a week, shops at Whole Foods like it's a hobby, and considers brunch a personality trait, this card was designed in a lab specifically for you. It earns 4x points on dining and groceries (up to $25K/year), which is genuinely elite. The $250 annual fee sounds steep until you realize there's $120 in Uber Cash credits built in. Do the math: $130 net fee for 4x on your two biggest spending categories. That's not charity, but it's close.

The 4x Categories (Why You're Here)

  • Dining: Restaurants, bars, cafes, fast food, delivery apps (DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub), meal kits
  • Groceries: Supermarkets (up to $25K/year, then 1x after)

The 3x Category

  • Flights: Booked directly with airlines (not third-party sites)

Everything Else: 1x

üõí Grocery Cap Explained

You earn 4x on the first $25,000 spent at groceries per year. After that, it drops to 1x for the rest of the year.

Reality check: $25K/year = $480/week in groceries. Unless you're feeding a family of 6 or shopping for a restaurant, you won't hit this cap.

üí∞ Real Earnings Example (Normal Person)

Let's say you spend:

  • $600/month on dining: 28,800 points/year
  • $400/month on groceries: 19,200 points/year
  • $1,000/year on flights: 3,000 points/year
  • Sign-up bonus (year 1): 60,000 points

Total first year: 111,000 points = $1,110+ in travel value

Minus the $130 net fee = $980 profit in year one. Not bad for eating food you were going to buy anyway.

The Credits (Use Them or You're Wasting Money)

$120 Uber Cash ($10/month)

What it is: $10 credited to your Uber account every month.

What you can use it for: Uber rides OR Uber Eats orders.

The catch: It doesn't roll over. Use it by month-end or it's gone.

Pro tip: If you don't Uber regularly, use it for Uber Eats. A $10 credit covers most delivery fees, effectively making delivery "free" once a month.

$84 Dining Credit (Limited Partners)

What it is: Up to $7/month back at select dining partners (Grubhub, Seamless, The Cheesecake Factory, Ruth's Chris, others).

The catch: You have to enroll and use specific merchants. It's not automatic like the Uber credit.

Reality check: Most people forget about this. If you use Grubhub anyway, great. If not, you're leaving $84 on the table.

Total Possible Credits: $204/year

If you max out both credits, your effective annual fee is $46. That's insane value. But only if you actually use them.

How to Redeem Points (Where Amex Shines)

Transfer to Partners (1-2+ cents/point)

Amex Membership Rewards points transfer 1:1 to 17 partners including:

  • Delta SkyMiles (best for domestic travel)
  • Air Canada Aeroplan
  • British Airways Avios
  • Virgin Atlantic
  • Hilton Honors (transfer at 1:2 ratio)
  • Marriott Bonvoy

If you know what you're doing, you can squeeze 2+ cents per point value. 60,000 points can easily become $1,200+ in business class flights.

Amex Travel Portal (1 cent/point)

Book flights, hotels, or rental cars at 1 cent per point. Simple, no complexity, decent value.

Statement Credit / Amazon (0.7-1 cent/point)

You can redeem for statement credits or use points at Amazon checkout. Don't. You're leaving 30-50% value on the table.

Bottom line: Transfer to partners or use the portal. Everything else is a bad deal.

Who Should Get This Card

Perfect for:

  • Foodies who dine out 3+ times per week
  • People who grocery shop at supermarkets (not Costco/Target)
  • Uber/Uber Eats users who'll max the $10/month credit
  • Travelers who want flexible points for international trips
  • People who already have the Amex Platinum (great combo)

Skip it if:

  • You rarely dine out or order delivery
  • You shop primarily at Walmart, Target, or Costco (not coded as grocery)
  • You won't use the Uber credit (making the fee $250 instead of $130)
  • You want a simple card with no credit tracking
  • You live somewhere Amex isn't widely accepted

Amex Gold vs. The Competition

Amex Gold vs. Chase Sapphire Preferred

Sapphire Preferred: $95 fee, 3x dining, 2x travel, better for general travel. Gold: $130 net fee, 4x dining/groceries, better for foodies. Winner: Gold if you spend heavily on food, Preferred for general travel.

Amex Gold vs. Capital One SavorOne

SavorOne: $0 fee, 3% dining/entertainment/groceries. Gold: $130 net fee, 4x dining/groceries, better transfer partners. Winner: SavorOne for casual users, Gold for optimizers.

Amex Gold vs. Citi Custom Cash

Custom Cash: $0 fee, 5% top category (up to $500/month). Gold: $130 fee, 4x dining/groceries unlimited. Winner: Custom Cash if under $500/month spend, Gold if over.

Bottom Line: Is Amex Gold Worth It?

If you eat out regularly and shop at supermarkets, the Amex Gold is one of the best value propositions in credit cards. 4x on your two biggest spending categories, $204 in potential credits, and 60K point bonus make the $130 net fee laughably low.

Get it if: You're a foodie, grocery shopper, or Uber user who'll maximize the credits.

Skip it if: You rarely dine out, shop at big-box stores, or won't use the Uber credit.

Our verdict: 4.7/5. The best dining/grocery card on the market. Highly recommended for foodies.
