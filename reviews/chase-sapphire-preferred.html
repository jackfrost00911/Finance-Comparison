Chase Sapphire Preferred Review 2025: The Goldilocks of Travel Cards

This is the sweet spot card: premium enough to feel worthwhile, affordable enough to not require justification to your partner. Who Should Get This Card

Perfect for:

  • People who travel 2-4 times per year (domestic or international)
  • Frequent diners who want rewards on restaurants
  • Anyone who wants flexible points that transfer to airlines/hotels
  • Chase ecosystem builders (pairs great with Freedom cards)
  • People who want premium perks without $400+ annual fees

Skip it if:

  • You never travel (get a cash back card instead)
  • You're over 5/24 (Chase will auto-deny you)
  • You want airport lounge access (upgrade to Reserve)
  • You spend under $5,000/year on travel and dining combined

How You Earn Points (The Good Stuff)

The Sapphire Preferred earns Chase Ultimate Rewards points, which are legitimately valuable‚Äînot airline miles that expire if you sneeze wrong.

Earning Rates

  • 5x points: Travel purchased through Chase Ultimate Rewards portal
  • 3x points: Dining (restaurants, delivery, takeout) + Streaming services
  • 2x points: Other travel purchases (flights, hotels, car rentals directly)
  • 1x points: Everything else (groceries, gas, shopping, etc.)

Earn points on those cards, then transfer them to your Sapphire Preferred for 25% bonus redemption value. Example Earnings

Let's say you spend:

  • $300/month dining = 10,800 points/year
  • $200/month travel = 4,800 points/year
  • $1,000/month other = 12,000 points/year
  • Total: 27,600 points/year = $345 in travel value

Add the 60K sign-up bonus and you're looking at 87,600 points in year one = over $1,000 in travel.

How to Redeem Points (Where the Magic Happens)

Chase Ultimate Rewards points have three main redemption options, ranked by value:

1. Transfer to Travel Partners (Best Value: 1-2+ cents per point)

Transfer points 1:1 to airlines like United, Southwest, Air France, British Airways, or hotels like Hyatt and Marriott. If you know what you're doing, you can squeeze 2-3 cents per point value. Example: 60,000 points = $1,200+ in business class flights if you transfer to the right partner.

2. Chase Travel Portal (Good Value: 1.25 cents per point)

Book directly through Chase's portal and get 25% bonus value. 60,000 points = $750 in travel bookings. Simple, no complexity, still better than cash. This is the move for 90% of people.

3. Cash Back or Gift Cards (Worst Value: 1 cent per point)

You can redeem for statement credits, direct deposits, or gift cards at 1 cent per point. Don't do this unless you're desperate. You're leaving 25-100%+ value on the table.

Travel Perks & Protections

The Sapphire Preferred includes solid travel insurance that actually matters:

  • Trip cancellation/interruption: Up to $10,000 per trip
  • Trip delay reimbursement: $500 per ticket if delayed 6+ hours
  • Baggage delay insurance: $100/day for essentials (up to 5 days)
  • Lost luggage reimbursement: Up to $3,000 per passenger
  • Auto rental collision damage waiver: Primary coverage (huge deal)
  • Purchase protection: 120 days, up to $10K per claim
  • Extended warranty: Adds 1 year to manufacturer warranty

The primary rental car insurance alone is worth the annual fee. Most cards offer secondary coverage, meaning your personal insurance pays first. Primary coverage means Chase pays first‚Äîyour rates don't go up.

How It Compares to Other Cards

Sapphire Preferred vs. Sapphire Reserve

Reserve costs $550/year but includes: $300 travel credit (net $250 fee), Priority Pass lounge access, 3x on dining/travel, 10x on Chase Travel, and premium car rental status. Worth it if you travel 6+ times/year. Not worth it if you travel 2-4 times.

Sapphire Preferred vs. Capital One Venture X

Venture X costs $395 but gives $300 annual credit + 10K anniversary miles, making it effectively free. Better value if you'll use the credits. Preferred is simpler if you won't.

Sapphire Preferred vs. Amex Gold

Amex Gold costs $250, earns 4x dining and groceries. Better for foodies, worse for general travel. Preferred is more flexible.

Bottom Line: Is It Worth It?

The Chase Sapphire Preferred is the best "first premium card" for a reason. It's not too expensive, not too complicated, and delivers real value without requiring a finance degree to optimize. If you travel even 2-3 times per year and dine out regularly, the $95 fee pays for itself.

Get it if: You want flexible travel rewards, reasonable annual fee, and solid perks without complexity.

Skip it if: You never travel, you're over 5/24, or you want lounge access (get the Reserve instead).

Our verdict: 4.9/5. One of the best all-around travel cards on the market. Highly recommended.
