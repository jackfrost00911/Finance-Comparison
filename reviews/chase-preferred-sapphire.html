Chase Sapphire Preferred Review

The Chase Sapphire Preferred card continues to be a favorite among casual travelers and points enthusiasts alike. But is it still worth the hype? Let's cut through the marketing fluff and see what CardSnark has to say—with sass and sarcasm.

The Sign-Up Bonus

Earn 60,000 points after spending $4,000 in the first 3 months. That sounds great—if you have $4,000 lying around you're willing to put on plastic without breaking a sweat.

Rewards Categories

5x points on travel booked through Chase, 3x on dining worldwide, and 1x on everything else. It's decent, but don't expect to get rich on your UberEats orders.

Fees and Rates

Annual fee of $95, waived the first year. Interest rates are typical for good-to-excellent credit scores, so don't expect miracles if you carry a balance.

Final Verdict

If you’re a newbie to travel rewards or want a solid middle-of-the-road card without the complications, this is it. Otherwise, there are flashier (and more complicated) cards out there for the ambitious spender.
