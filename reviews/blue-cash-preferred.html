Blue Cash Preferred® Card Review: The VIP Pass for Grocery Store Royalty and Binge Watchers

If you spend half your paycheck on groceries and the other half on Netflix, the Blue Cash Preferred® Card might just be your new spirit animal. It’s the only card that makes you feel good about compulsive snacking while you stream the latest overrated show.

What Rocks?

  • 6% cash back on U.S. supermarkets (up to $6,000/year)—that’s a whole lot of cartoon cereal, friend.
  • 6% cash back on U.S. streaming services—might as well get paid for your couch potato status.
  • 3% cash back on transit (taxis, rideshares, parking, trains)—commuting finally pays off. (Well, sort of.)
  • 3% back on U.S. gas stations—fuel up and don’t feel bad doing it.
  • 1% back on everything else—the afterthought, but hey, it’s something.
  • Welcome bonus: $250 after spending $3,000 in 6 months. Buy enough groceries and you’re there.

What Sucks?

  • $0 intro annual fee for the first year, then $95—ouch at renewal time.
  • 6% back cap on groceries disappears after $6,000. (Hello, limits.)
  • Some streaming platforms mysteriously “don’t count” (read the fine print or risk tears).
  • Amex isn’t accepted everywhere—leave your supermarket palace and it gets dicey.

So, Who Is This For?

This is the card for snackers, commuters, and hardcore binge-watchers—the “I’m not rich, but treat me like I am” crowd. If you don’t spend at supermarkets or have every streaming app, skip it.

The Bottom Line

The Blue Cash Preferred® Card rewards the truly average American lifestyle: eating, streaming, and griping about gas prices. It’s a must-have for maximizing everyday purchases. Just watch those annual fees or they’ll eat into your snack budget.
