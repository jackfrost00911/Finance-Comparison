How the 2% Cash Back Actually Works

The "double cash" name comes from Citi's clever (or annoying, depending on your perspective) earning structure:

  • 1% cash back when you make a purchase
  • 1% cash back when you pay off that purchase
  • Total: 2% cash back on everything

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Pay Your Balance in Full

If you carry a balance, you only earn the first 1% until you pay it down. This card rewards responsible behavior‚Äîpay your bill in full every month and you get the full 2%. Carry a balance and you're leaving money on the table (and paying 19% APR, which is infinitely worse than missing 1% cash back).

Earning Examples

Let's say you spend $2,000/month ($24,000/year):

  • Year 1 earnings: $480 cash back + $200 bonus = $680 total
  • Every year after: $480 cash back
  • Over 5 years: $2,400 + $200 bonus = $2,600 total

That's real money for doing literally nothing except using this card instead of a debit card.

How to Redeem Your Cash Back

Cash back redemption is refreshingly simple:

  • Statement credit: Reduces your balance directly (easiest option)
  • Direct deposit: Cash sent to your bank account
  • Check: Mailed to you (who does this anymore?)
  • Gift cards: Sometimes at a slight premium value

Minimum redemption is $25, which you'll hit after spending $1,250. Most people redeem every few months or set it to auto-redeem at $50.

Pro tip: If you have a Citi Premier or Prestige card, you can convert Double Cash rewards to ThankYou Points at 1:1 and then transfer to airline partners. But that's advanced stuff‚Äîmost people should just take the cash.

Citi Double Cash vs. Wells Fargo Active Cash

Functionally identical: both 2% flat, both $0 annual fee, both 3% foreign fees. Wells Fargo adds cell phone protection ($600 coverage) if you pay your bill with the card. Citi adds balance transfer options. Pick based on which bank you prefer (or which approves you).

Citi Double Cash vs. Alliant Cashback Visa

Alliant offers 2.5% flat but charges $99/year after year one. Do the math: you need to spend $20,000+/year for the extra 0.5% to justify the fee. Under $20K? Stick with Citi. Over $20K? Consider Alliant.

Citi Double Cash vs. Category Cards (Discover, Chase Freedom)

Category cards earn 5% on rotating categories but require activation and tracking. If you're organized and don't mind the mental overhead, pair this with a 5% category card. If you value simplicity, just use the Double Cash for everything.

Additional Perks (Spoiler: There Aren't Many)

The Citi Double Cash is a no-frills card. You're here for cash back, not airport lounges or concierge service. That said, it does include:

  • 0% intro APR for 18 months on balance transfers (3% fee)
  • Mastercard benefits: Extended warranty, price protection (terms apply)
  • Citi Entertainment: Presale tickets to concerts/events
  • No annual fee: Forever

That's it. No travel insurance, no rental car coverage, no purchase protection. If you need those perks, look at premium cards. This is a cash back workhorse, not a luxury vehicle.

Who Should Get This Card

Perfect for:

  • People who want maximum cash back with zero effort
  • Anyone allergic to tracking categories or quarterly activations
  • Minimalists who want one card for everything
  • People who pay their balance in full every month
  • Someone looking for a simple "set it and forget it" card

Skip it if:

  • You travel internationally (3% foreign fees will eat your rewards)
  • You carry a balance (you'll only earn 1% until you pay off)
  • You spend heavily in specific categories (dining, groceries) where 3-5% cards exist
  • You want travel perks, lounge access, or premium benefits
  • You're looking for prestige or status (this ain't it)

Pro Tips to Maximize This Card

1. Pair It with Category Cards

Use the Double Cash as your default card, then add:

  • Amex Blue Cash Everyday for 3% on groceries
  • Capital One SavorOne for 3% on dining
  • Discover it for 5% rotating categories

This setup covers 90% of spending at 2-5% back with minimal mental overhead.

2. Set Up Auto-Pay

To get the full 2%, you need to pay your balance. Set up auto-pay for the full statement balance to never miss the second 1% (and avoid interest).

3. Don't Travel With This Card

The 3% foreign transaction fee kills your 2% cash back. Use a no-foreign-fee card abroad (Chase Sapphire, Capital One, most travel cards).

4. Use the Balance Transfer Feature Strategically

If you have high-interest debt, the 18-month 0% APR offer can save you thousands. Just don't use this card for new purchases while carrying a transfer balance (new purchases get hit with interest immediately).

Bottom Line: Is the Citi Double Cash Worth It?

Yes. Unequivocally yes. Unless you travel internationally or carry a balance, this is one of the best no-annual-fee cards you can get. It's boring, but boring is good when it means free money for doing nothing.

Get it if: You want simple, reliable, maximum cash back with zero fees and zero complexity.

Skip it if: You travel abroad often, carry balances, or want premium perks.

Our verdict: 4.8/5. The gold standard for flat-rate cash back cards. Highly recommended.
